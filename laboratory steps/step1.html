Strawberry
🔨 Mechanical Mashing
🎙️ Lab Script:
"Place the strawberry in a plastic bag and seal it. Mash the fruit vigorously for 2 minutes. This mechanical force breaks the tough cellulose cell walls and pectin, exposing the cells for chemical treatment."
🧬 The Science: Strawberries are octoploid (8n), meaning they have eight sets of chromosomes, which is why they provide such a high, visible yield of DNA compared to other sources.

Onion
⚙️ Tissue Homogenization
🎙️ Lab Script:
"Finely mince the onion or use a blender to create a slurry. This increases the surface area significantly, allowing the lysis buffer to reach the nuclei of the fibrous plant tissues more effectively."
🧬 The Science: Onions have thick, fibrous cell walls. Without mechanical breakdown, the lysis buffer cannot penetrate deep enough into the tissue to release a significant amount of DNA.

🧫
Cheek Cells
🔬 Gentle Scraping + Protease
🎙️ Lab Script:
"Gently scrape the inside of your cheek with a sterile swab, then suspend cells in saline. Add proteinase K to digest histones and release DNA from chromatin without mechanical shearing."
🧬 The Science: Human cheek cells lack cell walls but contain tightly packed chromatin. Enzymatic digestion is crucial to liberate high-quality DNA while preserving integrity.
🧪 Start Cheek Cell Extraction →
