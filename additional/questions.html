📚 Model answer: Gel electrophoresis is a laboratory technique that separates DNA fragments by size using an electric field. The video mentions DNA fingerprinting (forensic analysis, paternity testing) as a real-world application where band patterns are compared.

✅ Model: DNA has a negatively charged phosphate backbone. When an electric field is applied, the negative charges are attracted to the positive electrode (anode), causing fragments to migrate through the gel.

🔬 Model: DNA is invisible to the naked eye. Staining (e.g., with fluorescent dyes like GelRed) binds to DNA, allowing visualization of distinct bands under UV light, so we can analyze fragment sizes and patterns.

🧬 Model: Short fragments migrate farther from the wells (towards the bottom of the gel), while long fragments remain closer to the wells due to slower movement through gel pores.

📊 Model: The sample whose bands have migrated farther from the wells (lower on the gel) contains overall shorter fragments compared to the sample with bands closer to the wells.

🧬 Model: By comparing the number and positions (size) of bands between two samples, identical or highly similar patterns indicate they likely come from the same individual or related sources, used in forensic or paternity analysis.
